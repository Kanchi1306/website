Stepping In Your 20's: Should It Feel Like A Race?

I just turned 20!!

While birthdays excite me, this one brought a quiet pressure — expectations, future plans, and the fear of falling behind.

So, I made myself a promise: no regrets this decade. I’ll explore everything that calls me.

Your 20s aren’t a race — they’re for discovering who you are and what you’d chase endlessly. The dream that keeps you up at night? That’s the one worth following. And if you don’t have a dream yet, for which you want to work hard, I bet, you have not explored much.

How Not to Fall Into the Rat Race?

Here’s how I’m trying to stay true to my path, and maybe this can help you too:

1. Find Yourself Through Creativity
Start painting, writing, dancing, building, designing — whatever feels like a spark.
When we create, we meet ourselves in raw, honest ways. It’s less about the result and more about the process of becoming.

2. Ask: Are Your Dreams Yours or Society’s?
Pause and reflect — are your actions aligned with what you want or what’s been silently imposed on you (even by those who love you)?
Often, we chase goals just because they’re familiar or “safe.” But safe doesn’t always mean right.

3. Be Willing to Go Against the Grain
If you realize your true dream lies outside society’s blueprint — dare to choose it anyway.
Because once you do, once you truly follow what calls your name, you’ll be in flow.
No stress. No anxiety. Just purpose.

So, in your 20’s don’t chase the timeline, chase what sets your soul on fire.
